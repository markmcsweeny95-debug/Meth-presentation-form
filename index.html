What is Crystal Meth?

Crystal methamphetamine is a powerful stimulant drug that affects the central nervous system.

It usually appears as clear crystal-like rocks or powder and can create intense bursts of energy and alertness.

Meth dramatically increases dopamine levels in the brain, which can create temporary feelings of confidence or euphoria. However, repeated use can quickly lead to severe addiction and dangerous physical and mental health effects.

Effects of Methamphetamine

Short-term effects can include:

• Increased energy
• Staying awake for long periods
• Reduced appetite
• Rapid heartbeat
• Anxiety and paranoia

Long-term use can seriously damage both physical and mental health. Users may experience hallucinations, severe addiction, memory loss, depression and major dental damage often called “meth mouth”.

Meth addiction can also destroy relationships, education, employment and financial stability.

Legal Status in the UK

Crystal meth is a Class A drug in the United Kingdom.

Possession can result in:
• Up to 7 years in prison
• An unlimited fine
• Or both

Supplying or producing methamphetamine is treated far more seriously and can result in life imprisonment.

Because of its high addiction risk and dangerous effects, UK police and health organisations treat methamphetamine as an extremely dangerous illegal substance.
