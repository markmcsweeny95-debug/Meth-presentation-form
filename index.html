Crystal Meth (Methamphetamine)

A 5-minute educational presentation for Sixth Form Form Time

What is it? Effects Risks Addiction Law Help

What is Crystal Meth?

Crystal methamphetamine is a powerful and illegal stimulant drug that affects the central nervous system. It usually appears as shiny, white or bluish crystals or powder.

It speeds up messages between the brain and body, creating intense but short-lived feelings of energy and euphoria.

Short-Term Effects

  • Extreme energy and alertness
  • Reduced appetite
  • Euphoria and confidence
  • Rapid heart rate and high blood pressure
  • Risky or impulsive behaviour
  • Insomnia (can stay awake for long periods)

Long-Term Effects

  • Severe addiction
  • Memory loss and brain damage
  • Tooth decay (“meth mouth”)
  • Weight loss and malnutrition
  • Anxiety, paranoia, hallucinations
  • Increased risk of heart failure or stroke

Why It Is Dangerous

Crystal meth is highly addictive after even short-term use. It can quickly change brain chemistry, making it extremely hard to stop once dependency develops.

Overdose risk increases with higher doses or repeated use over short periods (“binge use”).

Addiction

Meth affects dopamine levels in the brain, which control pleasure and reward. Over time, the brain produces less natural dopamine, meaning users feel low without the drug.

This cycle leads to strong cravings, compulsive use, and serious mental health decline.

Legal Status (UK)

Crystal meth is a Class A drug in the UK.

This means:
  • Possession can lead to up to 7 years in prison
  • Supply can lead to life imprisonment
  • Unlimited fines may also apply

Getting Help

If you or someone you know is struggling with drug use, support is available:
  • NHS drug support services
  • FRANK (confidential advice for young people in the UK)
  • School counsellors or safeguarding teams
  • Local drug and alcohol services

Asking for help early makes recovery much easier.
