What is Crystal Meth?

Crystal methamphetamine is a powerful illegal stimulant drug that affects the central nervous system.

It creates intense bursts of energy and alertness by increasing dopamine levels in the brain. However, these effects are temporary and come with serious physical and mental health risks.

Short-Term Effects

  • Extreme energy
  • High confidence
  • Reduced appetite
  • Staying awake for long periods
  • Rapid heart rate
  • Paranoia and anxiety
  • Dangerous impulsive behaviour
  • Risk of overdose

Long-Term Effects

  • Severe addiction
  • Memory and concentration problems
  • Weight loss and malnutrition
  • Tooth decay (“meth mouth”)
  • Depression and hallucinations
  • Heart and blood pressure problems

How It Affects The Brain

Meth massively increases dopamine — the brain chemical linked to pleasure and reward.

Over time, the brain struggles to produce dopamine naturally, making normal happiness difficult without the drug. This is one reason meth addiction becomes extremely powerful.

Addiction changes brain chemistry, behaviour and decision making.

Legal Status in the UK

Crystal meth is a Class A drug in the UK.

Possible penalties:
  • Possession: up to 7 years prison
  • Supplying: up to life imprisonment
  • Unlimited fines may apply
